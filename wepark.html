Parking difficulties are a common living problem faced by modern mega cities. Residents either need to suffer from high parking fees or spend a lot of time and energy in finding suitable parking spaces. At the same time, there are a large number of parking spaces that are unused. The utilization rate of parking spaces in mega cities is generally only between 40 - 60%. The government of Des Moines spends millions to build a garage, but was 92% empty in the middle of the day[1].

A study based on 15 blocks on the Upper West Side of Manhattan showed that drivers usually spend more than a third of mile to find an open space. Which means it will create about 366,000 vehicle miles of travel and 325 tons of carbon dioxide emissions.[2]

WePark is an intelligent parking solution based on these problems. The main user research and field study have been completed in Shanghai.

Based on the results of the previous study, WePark conducted further field research. During the field study, the actual Shanghai community was taken as an example to classify the types of surrounding buildings and the types of parking, and also make a rough estimate of the number of parking lots.
